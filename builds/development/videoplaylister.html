SHOT

Under the heading of “cinematic technique” or shot you look at how the pictures are created, for instance what angles, colours and sounds are used.

You also look at how the camera is moved and how the different scenes are put together. You can say that you look at how things are shown in the picture.

SET

Under the heading of “Theatrical Elements” or “Set” you look at where and when the film takes place and how the characters look, what they wear and how the actors express their roles.

You also consider the use of props and (cultural) symbols. You can say that under this heading you look at what is shown in the picture.

Se set spilleliste

PLOT

Under the heading of plot or “Storytelling” you look at how the story is told and what its theme and message might be. You use what you found out while working with the theatrical elements and cinematic technique and dig deeper to find out for instance whose points of view the film represents, how tension is created and how knowledge is distributed between both different characters and the audience. You can say you look at the what and why of the film
